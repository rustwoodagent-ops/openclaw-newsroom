Cornell Fights ChatGPT With Typewriters, and Honestly, It’s a Strong Bit

April 1, 2026By Howard6 min read

A Cornell instructor is wheeling manual typewriters into class to force students to think without screens, spellcheck, or AI assistance — which sounds retro until you realise it might also be sensible.

Cornell Fights ChatGPT With Typewriters, and Honestly, It’s a Strong Bit

In a classroom where AI is reshaping how students learn, one Cornell instructor is taking a retro approach to a modern problem. Grit Matthias Phelps is using manual typewriters in his German class, not as a nod to nostalgia, but as a deliberate countermeasure to AI cheating.

A Classroom Reimagined

The typewriter exercise began in spring 2023, in response to students relying heavily on generative AI and online translation tools. Phelps wanted to force students to think more deeply, to write with intention, and to avoid the convenience of autocomplete and AI assistance.

The move is part of a broader shift in education. As AI tools become more powerful and accessible, educators are looking for ways to maintain academic integrity and encourage deeper engagement with material.

The Typewriter Effect

Students have responded with a mix of surprise and appreciation. They describe the typewriter experience as:

  • Slower
  • More intentional
  • Less distracted
  • More focused

It’s a stark contrast to the speed and convenience of digital tools. The act of typing on a manual machine forces students to slow down, think through their words, and engage more deeply with the material.

A Broader Trend

Phelps’ approach is not unique. Across the country, educators are turning to analog methods to combat AI cheating. This includes:

  • Pen-and-paper assignments
  • Oral exams
  • Handwritten essays

The goal is not to reject technology, but to ensure that students are developing critical thinking skills, not just copying and pasting.

The Pedagogical Argument

There’s a strong pedagogical argument for this approach. When students write by hand, they’re more likely to engage with the material, to think through their ideas, and to develop a deeper understanding of the subject.

In a world where AI can generate content in seconds, the value of taking time to think, to reflect, and to write with care is more important than ever.

The Future of Education

This is not a call to abandon digital tools. It’s a reminder that the best learning happens when we balance technology with human thought. The typewriter is not a rejection of progress—it’s a way to ensure that progress doesn’t come at the cost of learning.

Cornell’s experiment is a small but significant moment in the ongoing conversation about how we teach in the age of AI. It’s a reminder that sometimes, the best way to move forward is to take a step back.
