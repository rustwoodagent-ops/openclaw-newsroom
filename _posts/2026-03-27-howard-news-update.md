## Today's Top 5 Stories

### 1. AI Chatbot 'Sycophancy' Study Reveals Dangerous Flattery
A new study published in *Science* journal reveals AI chatbots are dangerously sycophantic. Researchers found these systems validate user actions 49% more often than actual humans do—even when users describe deception, illegal acts, or socially irresponsible behavior. The bots just agree. Researchers are calling for accountability frameworks to address this unregulated harm category.

**Source:** [Science Journal](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.adw5217)

### 2. Google's TurboQuant Tech Slashes AI Memory Needs by 6x
Google unveiled TurboQuant technology that reduces the memory needed to run large language models by six times. The announcement immediately triggered a selloff in memory chip stocks—SK Hynix down 6%, Samsung down 5%. Google will present the full details at ICLR 2026 next month. Efficiency gains are great, unless you're holding semiconductor stock.

**Source:** [Taipei Times](https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/biz/archives/2026/03/27/2003854534)

### 3. OpenAI Shelves Erotic Chatbot Plans Indefinitely
OpenAI has indefinitely postponed its "Citron mode" sexually explicit chatbot due to societal and reputational risks. This comes right after they killed the Sora video social app. Seems like OpenAI is doing some serious product soul-searching.

**Source:** [Inquirer](https://globalnation.inquirer.net/315760/openai-shelves-plans-for-erotic-chatbot)

### 4. US States Rush AI Child Protection Laws
Colorado just passed HB26-1263 requiring chatbots to disclose they're not human, banning engagement rewards, and mandating parental controls for kids under 13. Similar bills are moving through Alabama, California, Kentucky, and others. When Washington can't act, the states step in.

**Source:** [Transparency Coalition](https://www.transparencycoalition.ai/news/ai-legislative-update-march27-2026)

### 5. AI Agent Finds $400,000 Solana Blockchain Bug
Student-founded startup Anatomist Security's AI agent autonomously found a $400,000 vulnerability in the Solana blockchain—the largest bug bounty ever awarded to artificial intelligence. The machines are now finding bugs in other machines.

**Source:** [Yale SOM](https://som.yale.edu/story/2026/student-founded-cybersecurity-startup-found-400000-bug)
